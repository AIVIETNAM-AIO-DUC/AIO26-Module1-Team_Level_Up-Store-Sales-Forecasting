Stage 4 · combine, decide, submit

Hybrid model & the actual submission

The final stage: combine the deterministic backbone (Stage 2) with the feature model (Stage 3), stitch the Stage 1 fallback back in for thin series, refit on the full training data, and write the 28,512-row submission.

Stage 4 · two specialists

The hybrid idea

Stage 2's deterministic backbone captures the slow drift and the calendar rhythm cleanly. Stage 3's feature model captures the short-horizon corrections — recent level, promotions, the earthquake window. A hybrid fits Stage 2 first, takes its residuals (what's left unexplained), and lets Stage 3 predict those. Two specialists, each on the problem it's good at.

Stage 4 · choose the level

Granularity decision

One model for all 1,782 series? One per family? One per store-family? Stage 4 picks the level using the holdout RMSLE — finer granularity captures local patterns but starves on data; coarser pools more data but smears patterns. The same five rules apply to every choice.

Stage 4 · Stage 1 returns

Fallback stitching

Thin series were peeled off before training and predicted by the Stage 1 fallback (seasonal-naive / near-zero). Stitch their predictions back in so the final output covers all 28,512 rows.

Stage 4 · the freshest data

Final fit on everything, then predict

Once the best approach is chosen on the holdout, re-fit the chosen model on the full training data (including the 16 holdout days — the freshest trend and seasonality live there) before predicting the real test.csv.
